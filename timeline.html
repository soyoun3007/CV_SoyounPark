1st year of my Ph.D.
Entered the endless cycle

Research is all about re-search. Yes, that's right. This is an endless cycle in a graduate school. You read a paper and try to reproduce it, but fail. Then, you find another paper hoping that it will work this time, but fail again. You find another and another until you ask yourself, "Why am I doing this?" Luckily, I was able to regain my strength to move forward, thanks to my PI and the lab.

2nd year of my Ph.D.
Survived from the comps

All the horrible words you can name - nervousness, exhaustion, stress, and fear - I felt during my doctoral comprehensive exam. After the exam, my brain was washed out, but only two surprising questions are still in my mind.
"Do you think artificial intelligence can replace you and your work?" "How do you think global warming affects bovine mastitis (my study topic)?"

3rd year of my Ph.D.
Broke the comfort zone

Participating in the Three Minute Thesis (3MT) competition was on my bucket list when I started my Ph.D. Communication skills are extremely important not only for within graduate school but also outside of academia. I was so happy to see myself overcoming my fear of public speaking.

Procrastinated
but happiest ever

Finally, I've finished all my lab work and been working remotely analyzing my data and writing manuscripts. I miss the lab, but my little Staphylococcus aureus is always with me.
